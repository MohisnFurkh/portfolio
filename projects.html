EfficientU-Net for Medical Image Segmentation

Developed a novel deep learning-based model for breast tumor segmentation and classification in ultrasound images. EfficientU-Net enhances the standard U-Net by incorporating EfficientNet as an encoder, which utilizes depth-wise separable convolution to maintain feature details while reducing computational complexity. Additionally, an atrous convolution (AC) block is integrated to capture multi-scale contextual information, improving segmentation accuracy. The model is evaluated on publicly available datasets (BUSI and Dataset B), demonstrating superior performance with fewer parameters, lower inference time, and higher accuracy than existing methods.

Deep Learning and Genetic Algorithm-based Ensemble Model

The study presents a hybrid deep learning and genetic algorithm-based ensemble model for feature selection and classification of BUS images. MobileNet is identified as the optimal deep-learning model for feature extraction due to its efficiency and high validation accuracy. The Genetic Algorithm (GA) is utilized for feature selection, optimizing the feature space to enhance classification performance. A weighted voting-based ensemble model combines multiple classifiers to improve diagnostic accuracy. Empirical evaluations on the BUSI and UDIAT datasets show significant improvements of approximately 4% and 9% in classification accuracy, demonstrating the practical value of this approach in breast cancer diagnosis.

UMA-Net with Multi-Scale Attention

This research proposes UMA-Net, an advanced U-Net-based architecture designed for BUS image segmentation, addressing challenges posed by frequency and scale variations. UMA-Net incorporates residual connections, attention mechanisms, and an atrous convolution-based bottleneck to capture multi-scale contextual information effectively. A novel adaptive ensemble loss function dynamically balances different loss components during training, optimizing key segmentation metrics.
